Breathe easy, study productively

Article reading time ~4 minutes

In our world, full of haste, stress and information noise, taking care of your own health and well-being comes to the fore. Fortunately, many keys to achieving harmony and productivity are at our fingertips, one of them is our breath.

Breathing practices based on a mindful approach to breathing have long been used in various cultures around the world to improve physical and mental health.

How does breathing affect our productivity?
• Reduce stress: Chronic stress negatively affects concentration, memory and learning ability. Breathing exercises, such as diaphragmatic breathing, activate the parasympathetic nervous system, which is responsible for relaxation, thereby reducing stress levels and creating an environment for productivity.
• Enhances cognitive function: Deep breathing has been proven to increase the flow of oxygen to the brain, which in turn improves concentration, memory, processing speed and creativity.
• Emotional regulation: Breathing practices teach us to consciously approach our emotions, not giving in to them, but managing them. This helps you stay calm in difficult situations, increases your resilience to stress, and helps you make smart decisions.
• Improving sleep quality: Healthy sleep is the key to a productive day. Breathing exercises before bed help relax the body and mind, which leads to faster falling asleep, sounder and restorative sleep.

What breathing practices are worth trying?
• Diaphragmatic breathing: This type of breathing uses the diaphragm, the main breathing muscle, to supply more oxygen to the body.
• 4-7-8 Practice: This simple technique involves counting from 4 to 7 while inhaling, holding the breath for 7 seconds and exhaling for 8 seconds.
• Anuloma Viloma (alternate nostril breathing): This technique helps balance the right and left hemispheres of the brain, which improves concentration and cognitive function.
All of the above techniques can be easily found on the Internet and you can learn more about their implementation. It is worth noting that there are many breathing practices and you can use them too.

Important!
• Regularity: The key to success is regular practice. Do breathing exercises 10-15 minutes a day, preferably in the morning or before bed.
• Combination with other practices: Breathing exercises can be combined with meditation, yoga, or other mindfulness practices for maximum benefit.
• Individual approach: Choose the techniques that suit you best and listen to your body.
Breathing practices are a simple but powerful tool that can become your faithful assistant on the path to productivity, well-being, and achieving your goals.
