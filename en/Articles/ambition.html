Goals always come first

Article reading time ~4 minutes

In today's world, where we are constantly bombarded with information and tasks, it is easy to lose focus and get bogged down. This is why setting goals and prioritizing becomes the key to productivity and achieving what you want.

Why set goals?
• Clarity: Goals help us define what we want and give us the impetus for our actions.
• Motivation: When we see a clear goal in front of us, it motivates us to move forward, even when we are lazy or difficult.
• Sense of achievement: Achieving a goal gives us a feeling of satisfaction and confidence in our abilities.
• Efficiency: Prioritizing allows us to focus on the most important tasks and avoid wasting time.

How to set goals?
• SMART: Goals should be Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant and Time-bound.
• Write it down: Write down your goals so they aren't just passing thoughts.
• Breakdown: Break big goals into smaller, manageable tasks.
• Visualization: Use vision boards or goal-setting apps to keep your goals in front of your eyes.

How to prioritize?
• Eisenhower Matrix: Divide tasks into categories of importance and urgency. Focus on the important and urgent tasks and then move on to the important but not urgent ones.
• ABC Method: Label tasks A, B and C based on their importance. Complete tasks A first, then B, then C.
• Intuition: Don't be afraid to listen to your intuition. If you think a task is more important than the matrix or ABC method makes it seem, do it first.

Remember:
• Goals are not a final point, but a path. Don't be afraid to adjust your goals as you go.
• Don't set too many goals at once. Focus on 2-3 main goals, and then add new ones.
• Track your progress. This will help you stay motivated and see your achievements.
• Don't be afraid of mistakes. Mistakes are part of the process.

Setting goals and prioritizing isn't a magic pill, but it is a powerful tool that can help you be more productive, get things done, and get your mind in order.

In addition to the above, remember:
• Visualization: Use vision maps, vision boards, or goal-setting apps to keep your goals in front of your eyes.
• Buddy: Find a friend, family member, or colleague who will support and motivate you.
• Reward: Reward yourself for achieving your goals.
• Review: Review your progress regularly and make adjustments to your goals and priorities.
