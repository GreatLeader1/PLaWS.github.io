Your worst enemy is procrastination

Article reading time ~3 minutes

Procrastination is putting things off until later, deliberately avoiding tasks that need to be completed. It can take different forms, but always has negative consequences.

Forms of procrastination:
• Perfectionism: the desire for perfection leads to the fact that a person is afraid to start work, because he is not sure that he can do it perfectly.
• Fear of failure: The fear of making a mistake or failing can paralyze the will and lead to procrastination.
• Imposter syndrome: a person does not believe in his own strengths and abilities, so he puts off tasks so as not to prove his “incompetence.”
• Plain laziness: reluctance to put in effort and work may simply be a result of fatigue, apathy or lack of motivation.

Why procrastination is terrible:
• Decreased productivity: Procrastination leads to tasks not being completed, goals not being achieved, and a person feeling unfulfilled.
• Stress and anxiety: Feelings of guilt and worry about undone things accumulate, leading to stress, apathy and even depression.
• Deterioration of self-esteem: a person who constantly puts things off begins to lose faith in himself and his strength.
• Problems in life: Procrastination can lead to problems in school, work, personal relationships and other areas of life.

How to deal with procrastination:
• Understand the reasons: Become aware of why you procrastinate. This will help you find ways to combat procrastination.
• Break tasks into small parts: Large tasks seem difficult and impossible, so divide them into several small steps.
• Set realistic goals: Don't set yourself goals that are too difficult to achieve.
• Eliminate distractions: turn off your phone, close unnecessary browser tabs, and find a quiet place to work.
• Motivate yourself: Find something that will motivate you to work.
• Plan: Make a work plan and follow it.
• Don't be afraid of mistakes: mistakes are part of the learning process. Don't be afraid to do them, but learn from them.
• Start with something small: Even if you don't feel like working at all, do something small, like start writing a text or read a few pages of a book.
• Reward yourself: Praise yourself for every step you complete and reward yourself for achievements.

Remember: procrastination is not a death sentence. You can and should fight it. Use these tips to defeat your worst enemy and become more productive. Do not give up! Fighting procrastination can be difficult, but it's worth it.
